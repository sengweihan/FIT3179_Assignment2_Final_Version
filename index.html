Suicide Cases Based on Age

The line chart on the left shows the total suicide cases for different ages over the years.

From there, we can see that overall, people with age between 35-54 years have the highest number of suicide cases throughout the year 1985 to 2015. I assumed that the reason for this might be due to them facing huge financial difficulties which makes them give up on their life to avoid paying the debt.

On the other hand, people with age between   5-14 years have the lowest number of suicide cases throughout the year 1985 to 2015 as compared to other ages. This does make sense because people with age between 5-14 years does not suffer from any mental health issues as well as financial issues.


Suicide Cases Based on Gender

From the chart, we can see that overall, the number of cases for males is more than for females over the years.

Based on research, although females attempt suicide at a higher rate as compared to males, they are more likely to use methods that are less lethal such as drug overdose and if they are noticed in the early stage by others, then they will have a chance to survive.

However, this is not the case for males as they tend to die by suicide via high-mortality actions such as hanging, carbon monoxide poisoning, and firearms.
